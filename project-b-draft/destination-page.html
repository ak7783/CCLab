Zeus: God of Thunder

Who is Zeus?

Zeus is the god of the sky in Ancient Greek mythology. He is considered a father and protector to the gods.

Symbolism

Zeus is commonly depicted holding a thunderbolt and an eagle. The lightening bolt he uses to impose his power, authority, and control over the skies and the natural forces. It represents Zeus's role as the god of the sky, thunder, lightning, and justice. Additionally, the thunderbolt serves as a symbol of divine punishment, as Zeus would use it to strike down those who defied him or threatened the order of the cosmos.

Family Overview

Zeus is the son of Cronus and Rhea, and he led his siblings in overthrowing their father, who had swallowed them at birth fearing a prophecy that one of his children would overthrow him. After defeating Cronus, Zeus and his siblings, Poseidon, Hades, Hera, Demeter, and Hestia, divided the cosmos among themselves, with Zeus ruling over the sky and heavens.

Fun Fact

In ancient mythology, Zeus is written to have created humans from clay.
